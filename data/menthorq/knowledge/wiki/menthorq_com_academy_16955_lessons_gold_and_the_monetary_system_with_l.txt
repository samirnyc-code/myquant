In this comprehensive discussion, we explore the current state of gold, monetary policy, and recent market turbulence with Luke Gromen, founder and president of Forest for the Trees. Luke specializes in connecting dots for investors to identify developing economic bottlenecks where excess returns accrue to different sectors.

The lesson examines the dramatic market moves following Donald Trump’s Liberation Day tariff announcements in early April. We saw historical rate-of-change movements across asset classes, including the 10-20 year Treasury yields nearly reaching 5% in just two and a half weeks—an extremely violent move for the bond market. The SPX traded down from 5,000 to bottom around 4,800, with some single-day moves not seen in decades.

Luke explains he wasn’t surprised by the market reaction of dollar down, bonds down, stocks down simultaneously. He had written for clients in late January that both Trump and Bessant essentially wanted to change the global monetary system and capital flows as they had existed for 50 years. The America First investment policy memo published February 21st essentially told China and other foreigners to take their money out of US financial assets, prompting Luke to turn very negative on NASDAQ.

A critical structural issue discussed is the Fed’s quasi fiscal deficit—the Fed is losing hundreds of billions of dollars annually because they’re upside down on their portfolio. This has never happened in U.S. history since the Fed’s founding in 1913. The last U.S. central bank to run a quasi fiscal deficit was the Confederate central bank during the Civil War, which resulted in hyperinflation. Luke emphasizes that quasi fiscal deficits are typically seen in Latin American banana republics with extremely high inflation and low central bank credibility—these are emerging market outcomes that result from playing emerging market games.

The discussion covers the 90-day tariff delay announcement that caused the SPX to surge 10% in a single day—a sign that something is severely broken in market structure. The unprecedented tension between Trump and Fed Chair Powell, including public criticism, represents a fundamental shift in confidence. Luke’s framework helps understand why current market dynamics represent not just short-term volatility but a fundamental restructuring of the dollar’s reserve status and global monetary system.
