In this lesson, you’ll learn how to analyze the current tariff situation between the US and China from a macro and data-driven perspective. We bring together experts from our macro team to discuss the impact of trade policies on markets, covering everything from volatility spikes to cross-asset correlations that matter for your trading decisions.

The discussion covers the current state of tariffs which are being imposed, threatened, and suspended across different countries. While Canada and Mexico represent the largest US trade partners due to existing treaties, China remains the biggest focus of tariff escalation. This week saw further developments with tariffs on China escalating from 125% to 145% to 200+%, with discussions of potentially reaching 300-400%. Markets have shown surprising calmness to recent announcements, possibly due to exhaustion from constant news flow.

The tariff uncertainty has created extreme volatility across multiple asset classes. Gold has surged dramatically, moving from around $3,000 to over $3,300, with a brief dip to $3,155 on April 2nd (when tariff announcements shocked markets) before rallying over $300. The gold rally coincides with intensified China tariffs and shows macro support from central bank buying by China, Japan, and potentially Russia as countries hedge against currency devaluation. The DXY (dollar index) has dropped significantly, falling below 100 with a decline of six to seven dollars in one and a half to two weeks, while the Euro strengthened to around 1.113-1.14.

Cross-asset impacts are critical to monitor for trading decisions. The 10:2 yield curve showed massive steepening since tariff announcements, with bond volatility reaching extreme levels—intraday moves of 45 ticks in the ZB future (30-year bond) compared to typical moves of 10-12 ticks. The stock market experienced severe pressure with near circuit breaker levels in futures. The primary markets discussed include the NASDAQ, S&P 500, and Hong Kong Hang Seng index, with particular focus on the US Tech sector and Chinese ADRs.

Practical trading considerations include watching how dollar weakness impacts US bonds, monitoring the correlation between tariff intensity and gold prices, and understanding that investors who started trading after 2020 likely haven’t experienced this level of volatility before. The tariff situation affects consumption expenditures, labor markets, and GDP growth, making it essential to track multiple asset classes simultaneously for comprehensive market understanding.
