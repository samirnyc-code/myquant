2 · The decomposition

What the panel numbers imply (1D Max, 2026-07-10)

From hold rate H, positive count P, broke-at-close Bc, comeback rate Cr:

N (total days)  = P / H                       = 348 / 0.8907 ≈ 391
closed beyond   = N × Bc                      = 391 × 0.109  ≈ 43
comeback days   = closed_beyond × Cr/(1−Cr)   = 43 × 0.43/0.57 ≈ 32
broke intraday  = comeback + closed_beyond    = 75
never reached   = N − broke_intraday          = 316

Self-check passes: never-reached + comeback = 316 + 32 = 348 = the displayed "positive outcomes." The headline 89% "hold" therefore mixes two very different things: days the level was never touched (~81%) and days it was touched but recovered (~8%).

Rests on two assumptions to confirm: "comeback rate" = comebacks ÷ intraday-breaks, and "broke" = any intraday pierce. Questions A1–A2 below pin these down.

3 · Questions for Fabio

Categorized, logically progressing

A. What the numbers actually count (definitions)

  1. "Broke during the day" — any intraday touch, a one-tick pierce, or a minimum distance beyond the level?
  2. "Comeback rate 43%" — denominator = days that broke intraday, or all regime days?
  3. "Broke at close 10.9%" — share of all regime days, or only of days that broke intraday?
  4. "348 positive outcomes" = closed back inside — does that include days the level was never reached, or only reached-and-held days?

B. Sample and denominator

  1. 348 at 89.07% implies ≈391 qualifying days — is that the denominator?
  2. How many of those days did price never reach the level? (Estimate: ~316, ≈81%.) Does the model track never-reached?
  3. Is the 3-year window rolling? Does the positive-outcome count change day to day?

C. Regime (the core question)

  1. How is regime defined — gamma positioning only, or also volatility, trend, dealer positioning, HVL…?
  2. How many historical days qualify as today's regime before the level-specific filter?
  3. Can subscribers see which regime today is classified as, even without the algorithm?

D. Timing and updates

  1. When are the stats generated — after the prior close, or pre-open?
  2. Do they update intraday as gamma levels move (Live tier)? How often?
  3. Do 20-minute-delayed subscribers see delayed stats too?

E. Bias and validation

  1. Is regime classified using only pre-open information, or can intraday/after-the-fact data enter it? (look-ahead)
  2. Are the probabilities out-of-sample, or an in-sample fit over the 3 years?

F. Transparency and access

  1. Why is only today's backtest visible? Can historical backtests be exposed?
  2. Could the outcome count link to the underlying dated occurrences (date / regime / level / result) so users can verify?

If sending a first message, lead with six: A1 (what counts as "broke"), A2 (comeback denominator), B5 (total sample), C8 (regime definition), D11–12 (when computed / intraday updates), F16 (historical access). The rest follow naturally from his answers.

4 · Comebacks

If he deflects

"It's proprietary."
I'm not asking for the formula — just the inputs and the denominator. That's methodology, not IP; every legitimate backtest states its universe and counting rule.
"Too technical / complicated."
Then just one number: how many total historical days is today's 89% computed from? A percentage with no sample size isn't interpretable.
"You don't need to know that."
I'm sizing positions on these probabilities. Whether 89% comes from 40 days or 400 — and whether it's conditional on the level being reached — materially changes the bet.
"Technical issues / can't show history."
Understood — then a CSV export of the dated occurrences behind one level? Or confirm I'm free to log them myself daily.
Full stonewall.
No problem — I'll keep my own daily record and check the calibration over a few months. (Polite signal that verification happens either way.)
