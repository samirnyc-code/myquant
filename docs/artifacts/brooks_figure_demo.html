High 2 / Low 2 Pullback

Two-legged (ABC) with-trend pullback to the 20-EMA -- Brooks' single most reliable with-trend day trade.
Context
An established trend pulls back in two legs (A, then the High1/Low1 bounce B, then final leg C) that breaks a minor trend line and ends at/near the 20-EMA (M2B in a bull, M2S in a bear). The prior High1/Low1 must have broken at least a minor trend line -- bar count alone is not a setup.
Entry
Buy stop 1 tick above the H2 signal-bar high (sell stop 1 tick below the L2 low). Aggressive traders use a limit at the prior bar's extreme.
Stop
1 tick beyond the signal bar; move to 1 tick beyond the entry bar once it closes as a trend bar. Do not tighten prematurely -- the true stop is the signal-bar extreme.
Manage
Scalp ~half at 1-2x risk (~4 ticks / 2 pts on the Emini), move to breakeven, swing the remainder and add on subsequent H2/L2. If it fails it often evolves into an H3/wedge or H4 -- re-enter larger on the stronger second signal.

Must-know rules

  • Demand earlier strength before you count the H2/L2 -- the pullback must follow a leg that broke a trend line or had a strong trend bar.
    “Note that the high 2 long was a terrible buy setup because five of the six prior bars were bear bars and the other bar was a doji. A high 2 alone is not a setup. There first has to be earlier strength.” Trends · p.p199
  • Trade the count only with the trend -- take H1-H4 only in a bull/range, L1-L4 only in a bear/range.
    “If the market is in a clear bull trend, do not look for low 1 or low 2 shorts, because those are setups only in bear trends and trading ranges.” Ranges · p.p108
  • Treat the M2B/M2S at the moving average as a top-tier entry.
    “A high or low 2 is one of the most reliable with-trend setups.” RPCBB · p.p79-119
  • In a strong trend expect the L2/L3 short to hit the stop beyond the prior higher low -- that reliability is why to take it with-trend.
    “In about 80 percent of cases, a low 2 or 3 short in a strong bear trend will hit the stops below the higher low... this is why low 2 and 3 shorts are so reliable in strong bear trends and high 2 and 3 longs are reliable in strong bull trends.” Ranges · p.p82-117

Don't trade it when…

  • A High 2 in a bear / Low 2 in a bull with no prior trend-line break is a trap -- it will fail and run the other way; reverse if it triggers an opposing signal.
    “if you ever buy early, always get out and even reverse if the market triggers a low 2 or 3 short, especially if there is a strong bear signal bar... If you are emotionally able to reverse, you usually should.” Ranges · p.p112
  • If the H2/L2 is really three-plus overlapping bars with a doji (barbwire), skip it and wait for more price action.
    “When there are three or more bars with a lot of overlap and one or more is a doji (Barb Wire), it is best to wait for more price action before initiating a trade.” RPCBB · p.p152
  • Do not fade with a Low 1/2 in a clear bull (or High 1/2 in a clear bear) -- those counts are just against-trend traps.
    “Although many are labeled, when the market is clearly trending up, you should not be looking to sell most Low 1 and Low 2 setups.” RPCBB · p.p147
