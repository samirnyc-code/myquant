Timeline

c. 2005–07 · High school
The newspaper in the back row

Reads Handelsblatt (he likens it to the WSJ) during class instead of listening; teachers permit it. Goal: become a bank CEO. Paper-trades German stocks for ~2 years off news.

2007 · Age 18
Opens first account with the car money

On his 18th birthday (earliest one can trade solo in Germany) he takes ~€20k his mother gave him for a car and driver's license, and secretly trades it. High per-order fees ($10–20) blunt early returns.

2007 · +3 months
Caught — but pays it all back

Mother asks about driving lessons; he admits he's trading, then returns the full sum from profit. Trust is not extended again. Trades German + US names, holds ≤1 week.

c. 2009
Abandons Germany for US penny stocks

Quits the German market ("if you're lucky, a 2% move") for US penny stocks and their 25–100% swings. A bank manager pushes him toward US markets: "you can lose faster and win faster."

c. 2010
The mentor — Oliver Willis

Gets two mentors, chiefly Oliver Willis — "the eye opener." Learns SMA 220 & 8, pattern reading, price action. Rejects all other indicators: "[expletive] off all the technical indicators."

2010s · The cycle
Blow up · pitch a backer · repeat

Blows up 50+ accounts. After each, he pitches wealthy people (network seeded by that bank manager): trades conservatively with their money, returns capital + profit within a month — then blows up his own account on "holidays." Concealed each personal blowup from backers.

2010s · Rock bottom
Homeless, in a car, disowned

Loses everything; homeless ~3 months living in his car. Family cuts contact — ~20 years of no contact with his mother. Their verdict on his ask for help: "it was your decision… you are not welcome." The lesson that stuck: cut losses fast.

Mid-2010s
The private hedge fund

Backers scale to 20–50 people; he forms a private, contracted, funded hedge fund and trades it 5–6 years without blowing up. Manages investor psychology deliberately ("I'm a sales guy").

c. 2023 · ep.260
From lone wolf to community

Joins the Chat With Traders community — his first real trading community. Married, on the Baltic coast, a 3-yr-old son and 1-yr-old daughter. Trading is "his lifestyle"; thinks about it 24 hours.

c. 2024
Community closes

The Chat With Traders community winds down after ~1 year. He calls the transition from "lonely wolf" to team player life-changing.

Jul 2023 · ep.262
Coaches a mentee into a prop-firm funded account

With Henrik Johansson, recaps "Beginner Trader to Funded Trader in 60 Days" — Patrick helped select a prop firm and pass its evaluation. Worth holding onto: two years later he'll publicly disparage exactly this.

May 28, 2025 · ep.300

Psychological Profile

Intuitive cognition core signature

Trades on a felt, non-verbal read of order flow he cannot articulate or teach. Pattern-recognition over rules; explicitly anti-analytical. Needs to "watch" the tape 5–10 min to sync before acting.

"I breathe the market in and I breathe the market out… I must be one-to-one with the stock market… I cannot describe my trading technique — it's more like a feeling."

Rebel / anti-institutional identity self-concept

Frames himself against convention and against regular work; rejected by family yet unwavering. Rebellion is central to his self-image, not incidental.

"I'm a rebel, 100 — more than 100. I'm not the average people… I cannot go to regular work."

Extreme risk appetite disposition

All-in, full financial power, a single instrument — watching two at once makes him "completely nervous." Claims ~5%/week and 3 all-in setups a day. Zero diversification by temperament, not strategy.

"When I have 100K I go all in… I can only watch one at one time."

Persuasion as a survival tool operating mode

Self-identifies as a "sales guy"; studied Grant Cardone / Tony Robbins. Repeatedly re-raised capital after ruin, and openly treats investor emotion as something to "play with."

"I play with this feeling… they will do what I want because I am the source of the money."

Antifragile resilience strength

Homelessness, 50+ blowups, family exile — each reframed as tuition. Notably, his pain function is not about money; it's about access to the game.

"It's not the pain to lose the money — it's the pain that you cannot trade."

Accountability-gated discipline key mechanism

The single most revealing pattern: conservative with other people's money (daily reporting kept him honest), reckless with his own (blew up on "holidays"). External accountability — not internal control — regulated his behavior. This is the load-bearing insight of the whole profile.

"With their money I play safe… I have to report every day. Alone, two–three months, then I blow up again."

Compulsive engagement intensity

Thinks about trading 24 hours a day; changes something in his method every single day. Restless adaptation — sometimes a strength (regime-fit), sometimes a symptom (no fixed, testable edge).

"Before I go to bed I think about trading… I change every day something in my style."

Contradictions & Risk Markers

A fair profile has to hold these next to the strengths — they're the parts a research read shouldn't launder.

⚑ Concealment vs. his transparency brand

He now demands "verified 5-year own-capital track records" and champions transparency — yet by his own account he repeatedly hid his personal blowups from the very backers funding him.

"I was never telling them again that I blew up my account… I not hope he listens to the podcast."

⚑ Survivorship narrative he now warns against

His origin method — all-in, one name, no risk management, pure intuition — is precisely the behavior he cautions new traders about in ep.300. The story that made him is the story he tells others not to live.

⚑ Externalized blame on the big loss

The one large investor loss is attributed to pressure from backers rather than his own sizing decision — an external locus of control at the exact moment accountability mattered most.

"It happened because they pushed me so hard… you were the one who pushed me to take more risk."

⚑ Unteachable-but-mentoring tension

Insists his edge is "100% not teachable," while simultaneously mentoring and building teaching products. What he actually transmits is likely process discipline and psychology — not the intuitive edge he claims is the source of returns.

Discord Cross-Check — the story doesn't match the footprint

Beyond the two podcasts, we hold 91 of his own posts from the MenthorQ Discord (daytraderpatrick): a journaling channel (Apr 6–15, 2026) and an MQ-analysis channel (Jun 9–26, 2026). Read against the interviews, they raise more questions than they answer.

⚑ The accounts are tiny

His own journal headers: 0DTE options account balance $1,229 (Apr 6), pre-market futures account balance $540 (Apr 7). These are hobbyist-sized accounts — not the footprint of a man who says he ran a funded hedge fund for 20–50 wealthy investors and compounded ~5% per week for 5–6 years. A trader with that record does not trade a $540 futures account in 2026.

⚑ A completely different trading method

Podcast Patrick: pure intuition, no indicators ("[expletive] off all the technical indicators"), all-in on one name, can't articulate his edge. Discord Patrick: highly systematic MenthorQ gamma-level confluence — GEX 1–5, HVL, Put Support, Call Resistance, 4H candle closes, round numbers, defined-range playbooks, written confluence tables. These are two different traders. The Discord version is, in effect, a MenthorQ product demonstrator — every journal is a levels advertisement (tagged @Free / @Premium / @Pro).

⚑ "One stock, I get nervous watching two" — vs. four instruments a day

He told ep.260 he can only watch a single instrument or he gets "completely nervous." Apr 8 journal: he traded MNQ + QQQ 0DTE options + crude oil — several instruments, same session, simultaneously. Directly contradicts the stated psychology.

⚑ Return math that doesn't reconcile

Weekly claim: ">$7,500 this week… less than 1 hour total active trading time" — on accounts showing $540 and $1,229 balances (that's a several-hundred-percent weekly return, the same physically implausible magnitude as the podcast's "5%/week for years"). And the account-status card is internally broken: "All-Time P/L +$2,156 (+2.2%)" while the account balance is also stated as $2,156 — a +2.2% and a +100% reading of the same number. Numbers look templated for marketing, not booked.

⚑ "No overnight positions" — then a midnight stop-order hold

Ep.260: "no overnight position, the risk is too much." Apr 7 journal: places a buy-stop above the market near midnight Berlin to catch a ceasefire pop and goes to bed with the order live. Small, but another seam between the stated rules and the shown behavior.

To be fair to him: the journals are explicitly trading-room marketing content, the small accounts could be deliberate "teaching" accounts, and he does post a losing day (Apr 13, −$458) under a "nothing hidden" banner. But the core tension stands — the 2026 footprint is a small-account MenthorQ room host running a systematic levels method, which is a different person than the intuitive, all-in, hedge-fund-running penny-stock scalper of the origin story. No verified track record reconciles the two.

⚑ The review complaint that should concern us most

The recurring negative theme isn't billing — it's the levels. Multiple reviewers: the numbers "line up great with previous days' market but realtime was wrong on more than one occasion" and "did more damage than good." That is precisely the hindsight-fits / forward-fails failure mode our own forward-reveal marking tool and no-lookahead touch study were built to catch. ⚑ "Business partner" vs. the org chart

Fabio reportedly called Patrick a "business partner." But MenthorQ's own About-Us lists Patrick as a "community mentor" (alongside Maciej) — not a co-founder or executive. The founders are Fabio and Marco. "Business partner" is informal/brand framing, not the company's formal structure.
