Order flow · what OHLC can't see

Every number a footprint bar knows about the fight for price.

A candle tells you where price went. A footprint tells you who did the work to get it there — how much volume hit the bid vs the ask at every single price, who was aggressive, who got absorbed, and where the auction was left unfinished. Below is every field we can read straight out of the mzFootprint indicator on your chart — ~90 data points, all public, all free — grouped, explained, and shown with a worked example.

The mental model

One bar is a ladder of prices. At each price sit two numbers: volume that traded at the bid (aggressive sellers) and at the ask (aggressive buyers). Everything else — delta, POC, imbalance, absorption — is just a pattern read off that ladder. Learn to see the ladder and the 90 fields become obvious.

Read it row by row. This bar traded 7536–7540.

POC = 7538 — the most-traded price (905+870). Fair value for the bar; price gravitates back to it.

Ask imbalance at 7539 — 640 bought vs the 210 sold one row below: aggressive buyers stacking. Real upward intent.

Absorption at 7537 — 1180 hit the bid but price didn't fall: a passive buyer absorbed the selling. Sellers trapped → bounce risk.

Delta = total ask − bid = (180+640+870+240+90) − (120+210+905+1180+160) = −555. Net sellers, even though buyers were loud at 7539. That tension is the edge.

Reading 7/16 with this

The whole point. On 7/16 price broke HVL 7535 and we bought a straddle (needs a big move) — it lost, because the break was an orderly drift, not a violent one. OHLC couldn't tell those apart at the moment of the break. The footprint can. Here's exactly what we'd check on the bars that broke 7535:

The one question: was the HVL break real or hollow?

Real break → straddle / put debit

Stacked sell-side imbalances punching down through 7535, negative delta confirming, a new orderflow low, no absorption catching it. Momentum is real → long vol pays.

Hollow break → put credit / condor

Absorption at 7535 (big bid volume, price won't progress), delta divergence (price lower, delta higher), unfinished-auction magnets above. Drift, not crash → the straddle was the wrong vehicle; sell premium instead.
