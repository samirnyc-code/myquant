## Summary
Short annotated CL 02-19 case study (M5 context, M3 detail) showing that a cluster of Big Trades can create a support zone. After several Big Trades print, the price decline stops; the M3 chart with mzFootprint reveals the institutional accumulation, and the "Big Trades Zone" then works as a support area. Illustrates order-flow recognition rather than mechanical rules.

## Key concepts & definitions
- "Big Trades" Zone: a price band containing a cluster of large executed transactions, treated as a support area.
- mzBigTrade detects institutional-sized trades; mzFootprint shows granular per-price order flow.
- M5 for broader context, M3 + footprint for accumulation detail; support validated when the decline halts at the zone.

## Rules / thresholds / settings
None given — no size thresholds, indicator parameters, entries, stops, or targets.

## Order-flow signature described
Several large prints cluster in a narrow zone during a decline; downside momentum stops at the zone (large executed size with no further price progress), which subsequently holds as support.
