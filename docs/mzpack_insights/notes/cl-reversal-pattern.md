## Summary
CL (crude oil) M30 reversal pattern example using mzFootprint and mzVolumeProfile. The pattern is a three-stage sequence: (1) a big negative delta bar with high volume marks the bottom of a range (selling climax), (2) a range/consolidation forms, and (3) a big positive delta bar with high volume signals the range breakout upward and provides the entry. No numeric thresholds are given; the pattern is shown visually.

## Key concepts & definitions
- Range bottom signal: "big negative DELTA with high VOLUME" at the low — climactic selling that defines the range floor.
- Range formation: consolidation phase following the climax.
- Range Breakout Signal: "big positive DELTA with high VOLUME" — aggressive buying confirming the reversal/breakout.
- Indicators: mzFootprint (per-price delta/volume), mzVolumeProfile (range structure).

## Rules / thresholds / settings
None given — no exact delta values, volume levels, entry price, stop, or target.

## Order-flow signature described
Selling climax: extreme negative delta on high volume at the low with no follow-through (price ranges instead of continuing down), then extreme positive delta on high volume as buyers take control and break the range.

## Relevance to our research
Effort-vs-result template: climactic negative delta at a low that fails to produce continuation is our absorption-at-extremes signature; the delta-flip breakout is a natural CVD-based confirmation to test on ES.
