## Summary
Short annotated long trade built around a "Mirror Level" prerequisite. Once the mirror level occurred, the trade was validated microstructurally by the mzBigTrade indicator showing pressure on the BID side at the day's VAL, then confirmed by a big aggressive BUY trade at the local low. Long taken with stop under the support zone and target at the day POC (R:R 1.6). A downloadable workspace is referenced; the article itself does not define "mirror level" explicitly.

## Key concepts & definitions
- Mirror Level: not explicitly defined in the article; used as the prerequisite condition ("Once the Mirror Level occurred"). (In common order-flow usage, a prior resistance/support flipping roles — but the article does not state this; do not attribute.)
- Confirmation chain: (1) mirror level occurrence, (2) mzBigTrade pressure on BID side at day VAL, (3) big aggressive BUY trade at local low.
- VAL (Value Area Low) of the day used as the location for the setup; day POC as the target.

## Rules / thresholds / settings
- Stop loss: under the support zone.
- Take profit: day POC.
- Risk-reward: 1.6.
- No numeric indicator thresholds, chart settings, or parameters given.

## Order-flow signature described
At the day VAL: bid-side pressure detected by mzBigTrade (large trades hitting the bid without price giving way), then a large aggressive BUY print at the local low signaling responsive buyers taking control.

## Relevance to our research
Directly analogous to our defended-level and VP-touch studies: sell pressure into the bid at VAL with no downside progress (absorption) followed by aggressive buying, targeting the POC retest. Good template for a level-touch outcome label.
