## Summary
Annotated 6E 03-19 (euro FX futures) trade example on M5 and M15 charts using mzVolumeDelta, mzVolumeProfile, and mzFootprint. A huge up-bar with extreme volume and big positive delta establishes momentum; a "Stop Area" at 1.14630–1.14650 coincides with the session Value Area High (VAH). Price tests this VAH resistance three consecutive times and is rejected, with the footprint used to inspect order flow at the level.

## Key concepts & definitions
- Stop Area: a price zone where an advance stalls, here aligned with the session VAH.
- VAH (Value Area High) from the session volume profile used as the key resistance reference.
- Extreme-volume, big-positive-delta up-bar as the initiating context; footprint used to read order flow at the tested level.

## Rules / thresholds / settings
- Stop Area / VAH: 1.14630–1.14650.
- Three consecutive tests of the VAH zone.
- Timeframes: M5 and M15. No other explicit parameters, entries, stops, or targets given.

## Order-flow signature described
Big positive delta on an extreme-volume bar into resistance, then repeated (3x) tests of a narrow VAH band without acceptance — effort into the level with no upside result; footprint shows the per-price action at the zone.

## Relevance to our research
Directly relevant to VP-level touch outcomes (VAH retests, multiple-touch behavior) and effort-vs-result absorption at extremes — a clean template for our level-touch outcome labeling at VAH/VAL.
