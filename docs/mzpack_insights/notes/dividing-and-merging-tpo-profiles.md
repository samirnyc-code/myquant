## Summary
Explains the analytical use of merging TPO profiles (combining multiple sessions into one macro profile) versus dividing them (segmenting into smaller intraday windows), via the mzVolumeProfile indicator. Merged profiles reveal long-term value areas, major POCs, and overall structure; divided profiles expose session-specific behavior (e.g., European vs. US session, opening range, midday reversals). The recommended workflow aligns macro structure from merged profiles with micro timing from divided ones, including filtering false breakouts by cross-referencing the two.

## Key concepts & definitions
- TPO profile: visualizes price levels against time spent at those levels (mzVolumeProfile).
- Merging: combining multiple sessions' data for a macro view — "long-term trends, key support/resistance levels, and the overall market sentiment"; pinpoints "long-term value, such as major Points of Control (POCs) or value areas where price has repeatedly balanced."
- Dividing: segmenting into smaller timeframes to reveal "intraday or session-specific trends" and short-term price movements.
- Macro-micro alignment: trade with the broader trend (merged) while timing entries from short-term shifts (divided).
- Breakout confirmation: use both to filter "false breakouts by cross-referencing the broader market structure with short-term price reactions."
- Trader profiles: swing/position traders lean on merging; day traders on dividing.
