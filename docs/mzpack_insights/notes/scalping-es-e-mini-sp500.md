## Summary
Video-based ES scalp example using mzMarketDepth and mzAggressiveTrade within a TPO/Market Profile context. The session is read as a probable DD-day type on the TPO profile. Entry is a long triggered by a 1000-lot aggressive BUY trade near a support level just above a single-print area on the TPO profile. mzMarketDepth confirms the institutional activity; single prints mark the structural support. The author notes that prior trades can (adversely) influence decisions on subsequent setups.

## Key concepts & definitions
- TPO day-type context: probable "DD-day" type identified on the TPO profile before trading.
- Single-print area: low-participation TPO zone acting as structural support/reversal reference.
- Aggressive trade: large market-order execution (detected by mzAggressiveTrade) as entry trigger.
- Behavioral note: "sometimes our previous trades affects on next one."

## Rules / thresholds / settings
- Entry example: 1000-lot aggressive BUY near support, just above the TPO single-print area. No configured thresholds, exits, or stops given.

## Order-flow signature described
A very large aggressive buy print landing at structural support (just above single prints), with DOM (mzMarketDepth) showing supportive depth — initiative buying at a defended structural level.

## Relevance to our research
Relevant to defended levels and large-print triggers: big aggressive prints at profile-structure levels (single prints, value edges) as touch-outcome predictors in our ES footprint data.
