## Summary
Brief conceptual trend-pullback scalping method for ES on a 400-tick chart with mzVolumeProfile and mzVolumeDelta. Four steps: determine trend direction (example is an uptrend); spot a negative delta cluster during the pullback; wait for a big positive delta in a Volume Profile positioned within some range of the pullback low and some bar distance from it; enter long on the Volume Profile close. Exact ranges/distances are left unspecified.

## Key concepts & definitions
- With-trend pullback entry: counter-trend (negative) delta cluster marks the pullback; resumption is confirmed by big positive delta in the profile.
- Positive VP condition: the positive-delta profile "must be in some range from low of the pullback and on some distance measured in bars."
- Entry trigger: long on Volume Profile close (i.e., when the confirming profile bar completes).
- Indicators: mzVolumeProfile (volume/delta distribution), mzVolumeDelta (directional flow).

## Rules / thresholds / settings
- Chart: 400-tick ES. Otherwise none given — no numeric delta thresholds, range sizes, or bar distances; no stops/targets.

## Order-flow signature described
In an uptrend: a cluster of negative delta on the pullback (sellers pressing) that fails to extend, followed by a big positive delta print near the pullback low signaling buyers resuming control.

## Relevance to our research
General background for CVD/delta-flip continuation logic: negative-delta pullback + positive-delta reclaim is a testable with-trend continuation signature in our footprint data.
