## Summary
Annotated GC 02-19 (Gold) long (author: Konstantin/KO) on M5 and M3 charts using four MZpack indicators: mzBigTrade, mzFootprint, mzVolumeProfile and mzVolumeDelta. Context was a downtrend with heavy institutional selling. The setup tracked a two-phase Big Trade shift: Area 1 where large sell orders exceeded large buys, then Area 2 where large buys exceeded large sells — the reversal cue. mzFootprint marked the support zones where price was stopped. The long was closed near the VAH, taking Area 2 (Big Trades Buy Volume > Big Trades Sell Volume) into consideration.

## Key concepts & definitions
- Two-phase Big Trade regime: Area 1 = Big Trade sell volume > buy volume (downtrend pressure); Area 2 = Big Trade buy volume > sell volume (reversal/momentum shift).
- mzBigTrade as the primary signal — direction of institutional order flow via large-trade imbalance.
- mzFootprint support zones: price levels "where the price was stopped" — historically defended by buyers.
- Exit at VAH aligned with the Area 2 buy-dominance confirmation.

## Rules / thresholds / settings
None given — no indicator settings, size thresholds for "big" trades, or quantified parameters disclosed.

## Order-flow signature described
Downtrend with dominant large sells stalling at footprint-identified support (price stopped despite selling), then the large-trade imbalance flipping to buy-dominant (Area 2) and carrying price to VAH.

## Relevance to our research
Large-trade imbalance flip at a defended footprint support level, resolved by a run to VAH — supports our defended-level detection and suggests a Big-Trade (large-lot) imbalance regime feature for touch-outcome models.
