one weak signal, three nulls

Nine of ten tested claims are dead. The single survivor is H1: price is rejected at Call Resistance more often than at a geometrically identical placebo level — but it rests on n=66 resolved touches and the control design had to be repaired twice before it ran. Treat it as a hypothesis worth one more round, not an edge to size.

Why the placebo is the right control

The placebo takes the CR's offset from the open from a different randomly chosen session and applies it to today's open. Same distance geometry, same session, wrong day's gamma. If a barrier N points above the open simply tends to reject, the placebo scores the same and the difference vanishes. It doesn't — that gap is the whole result.

Design repairs — logged, because they weaken the result

Two changes were made after pre-registration. Both were made before any effect direction was visible, which is the only reason they're defensible — but they still widen the garden of forking paths and are a reason to discount H1.

  1. Span 1.5% → 3.0%, exclusion 15 → 12pts. The original control pool collapsed to 0.72 strikes/session with 594 sessions at zero. Broken instrument, not a result.
  2. Same-day grid control → date-shuffle placebo. Even widened, a 12pt exclusion around ~14 published levels evacuates the near-open region where every touch happens; grid controls sat 60–140pts out and were never touched. The placebo fixed this and also killed E3's false positive.

What I'd do next — and what I would not

Would not: size anything on H1. n=66, one repaired design, effect concentrated in a single VIX tercile.

Would: (1) widen the sample — same test on NQ/RTY, whose MQ histories are already pulled, giving 3 quasi-independent replications rather than a bigger n on one instrument; (2) test whether the rejection is visible in order flow at the level (the footprint pipeline can do this, but currently holds one session — it needs a backfill first); (3) only then, if it survives both, define a Brooks failed-breakout entry at CR and chart-audit every fill before any P&L is claimed.
